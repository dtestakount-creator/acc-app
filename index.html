How ACC Safety Rating Works

SA is the most important rating in ACC. It measures your ability to avoid causing incidents — contact, dangerous rejoins, and forcing cars off track all reduce it. Clean, zero-contact races raise it. There is no shortcut.

The SA ladder
0–29
Danger Zone
Do not race. Practice only. Zero contact is the entire goal.
30–54
Building
Short races, low AI aggression. Follow, don't fight.
55–74
Solid
Deliberate overtakes only. SA discipline under pressure.
75–100
Elite
Full wheel-to-wheel with zero compromises. The SA benchmark.
The 4 SA commandments
GAPIf the gap closes before your braking point — back out. Every time. No exceptions.
T1Lap 1 Turn 1 is a trap. Take the outside line or wait. One position costs nothing vs SA loss.
LINESWhite lines are absolute limits. Track limit violations stack up. Stay inside them.
REJOINAfter any off-track, slow down completely before merging. A second incident costs double.

How ACC Pace & Consistency Work

Pace (PC) measures how close your lap times are to the theoretical optimum for your car and track. Consistency (CN) measures how repeatable your pace is across a stint — tight delta between laps.

The pace hierarchy
1. Consistency first
A 1:55 every lap beats a 1:52 on lap 1 followed by a 2:01 on lap 4. Consistency wins races and builds CN faster than chasing outright speed.
2. Sector commitment
70% of lap time gain lives in Sector 2 (technical middle). Attack one corner per lap in practice. Never try to fix the whole lap at once.
3. Trail-braking unlocks the next 0.5s
Releasing the brakes to zero before turn-in loses time at every medium-speed corner. Learn to carry light brake pressure through the first 20% of corner entry.
4. Last corner exit = lap time
The exit from the final corner before the main straight is worth more than any other corner. Sacrifice entry position for maximum exit speed onto the straight.
Delta targets by CN rating
CN below 40Delta > 2.5s — focus entirely on consistency
CN 40–60Delta 1.0–2.5s — repeatable braking points
CN 60–75Delta 0.5–1.0s — sector-level optimisation
CN 75+Delta <0.5s — peak lap commitment
